Projects

Selected research, lab initiatives, and tools shaped by remote sensing and geospatial analytics.

Research spotlights

Satellite Anthrax Surveillance

Master's thesis mapping high-risk landscapes in the Texas Anthrax Triangle by combining multispectral satellite data, phenology trends, and cloud computing. Provides early-warning indicators for wildlife health teams.

UAS Imaging of Cataloochee Valley

Led multi-sensor flights across Great Smoky Mountains National Park in support of NSF RAPID Grant #2501466, delivering orthomosaics and surface models to track habitat recovery.

Maritime Detection for Homeland Security

Developed TensorFlow-based vessel detection and tracking for Oak Ridge National Laboratory, supporting U.S. Coast Guard interdiction missions with faster intelligence cycles.

Land Cover Change Diagnostics

Engineered cloud pipelines to analyze high-resolution land cover and phenological signatures linked to infectious disease spread, pairing remote sensing outputs with field sampling.

Tools and automation

I build reusable codebases that help teams collect, process, and analyze geospatial data without reinventing the wheel.

  • MAESon — an open-source toolkit (in active development) for raster and point cloud analysis, designed to plug into research lab workflows.
  • UAS mission packages — repeatable checklists, payload configs, and processing scripts that reduce error during rapid response flights.
  • Lab automation scripts — Python utilities that keep UT's GIS labs patched, imaged, and ready for back-to-back teaching sessions.
